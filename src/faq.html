Frequently Asked Questions

Home Frequently Asked Question

Why Action Against Islamophobials needed in the community.

Reporting incidents of Islamophobia not only helps the victim seek justice, but it also raises awareness about the prevalence and severity of such incidents. Additionally, it aids in the collection of accurate data, which is essential for informing policy decisions and developing effective strategies to address anti-Muslim, anti-Arab and anti-Palestinian racism.

Why should I report an incident to AAI?

Thanks to thousands of people like you who have reported incidents, we are able to help communities across the country by reporting on trends, educating lawmakers and law enforcement and advocating for stronger protections from incidents and crimes. AAI works to prevent people from being targeted because of their religion, race, ethnicity, gender, sexual orientation, gender identity, national origin or level of ability. But we cannot do it alone. We need your help as, together, we work toward making our communities, country and world a safer place for all.

Is my personal information shared with others?

Your personal information is kept strictly confidential. AAI only shares anonymized data for statistical and advocacy purposes. We never share your personal details without your explicit consent.

Does AAI have any services that can help mitigate incidents?

Yes, AAI offers various support services including legal assistance, counseling referrals, community education workshops, and advocacy with local authorities. Our team can help connect you with the appropriate resources based on your specific situation.

When should I call the Police?

You should call the police immediately if you are in danger or if a crime has been committed. In emergency situations, always dial emergency services (000) first, then report the incident to AAI afterward. For non-emergency situations where you've experienced discrimination or harassment, you can report to AAI first and we can help guide you on next steps.

I am a minor under the age of 18, can I still report on AAI?

Yes, minors can report incidents to AAI. We encourage you to have a parent or guardian assist you with the reporting process, but we understand this isn't always possible. Our team is trained to handle reports from minors with extra care and sensitivity.

Once I've reported my incident, when does AAI call back?

Our team typically responds to reports within 24-48 hours during business days. For urgent matters, we prioritize response times. When you submit a report, you'll receive an automatic confirmation, and a team member will follow up personally as soon as possible.

Any other questions you want to add

If you have any other questions not covered here, please don't hesitate to contact us directly. You can reach our support team via email at support@aai.org or by phone at 1800-AAI-HELP during business hours.
